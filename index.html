Hyderabadi Kacche Gosht ki Biryani

🍲 Cooking Instructions

  1. Wash and soak basmati rice for 30 minutes, then drain.
  2. Marinate mutton with yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, green chilies, spices, and fried onions for at least 1 hour.
  3. In a heavy-bottomed vessel, spread marinated mutton at the bottom.
  4. Layer soaked rice on top of the mutton.
  5. Add saffron milk, ghee, and fried onions over the rice.
  6. Cover with a tight lid and cook on low flame (dum) for 40–45 minutes.
  7. Switch off the flame, let it rest for 10 minutes before opening.
  8. Gently mix and serve hot with raita or mirchi ka salan.

Karnataka's Bisi Bele Bath

🍲 Cooking Instructions

  1. Wash 1 cup rice and ½ cup toor dal, then pressure cook together with 4 cups of water until soft and mushy.
  2. Soak a small lemon-sized tamarind ball in warm water for 10 minutes and extract the pulp.
  3. Chop and boil 1 cup of mixed vegetables like carrots, beans, peas, and capsicum until tender.
  4. In a pan, dry roast chana dal, urad dal, coriander seeds, cumin, dry red chilies, curry leaves, and methi seeds, then grind to a fine powder (Bisi Bele Bath powder).
  5. In a large pot, heat 2 tbsp ghee, add cooked dal–rice mixture, boiled veggies, tamarind pulp, and 2–3 tsp of the spice powder.
  6. Add salt and a small piece of jaggery for balance; adjust water for a slightly runny consistency.
  7. Simmer the mixture on low flame for 8–10 minutes so flavors blend well.
  8. Prepare tempering with ghee, mustard seeds, curry leaves, hing, and cashews, then pour it over the Bisi Bele Bath.
  9. Mix gently and serve hot with papad, boondi, or ghee on top.

Kerala Appam

🍲 Cooking Instructions

  1. Wash and soak 1 cup raw rice for 4–5 hours, then drain and grind with ¼ cup grated coconut, 2 tbsp cooked rice, and water to make a smooth batter.
  2. Add ½ tsp dry yeast dissolved in 2 tbsp warm water and 1 tsp sugar to the batter.
  3. Mix well and let it ferment overnight or for 8 hours in a warm place.
  4. After fermentation, add salt and a little water to adjust consistency (slightly thinner than dosa batter).
  5. Heat an appam pan or small wok and grease lightly with oil.
  6. Pour a ladle of batter into the center and swirl the pan in a circular motion to spread it thin on the edges and thick in the center.
  7. Cover with a lid and cook on low flame for 2–3 minutes until the edges turn crisp and the center is soft.
  8. Do not flip; remove gently once cooked.
  9. Serve hot with vegetable stew or coconut milk.

Chennai Style Masala Dosa

🍲 Cooking Instructions

  1. Soak 2 cups rice and ½ cup urad dal separately for 4–5 hours, then grind them together with a little water into a smooth batter.
  2. Add salt, mix well, and let the batter ferment overnight or for 8–10 hours.
  3. Boil 2–3 potatoes, peel, and mash them for the masala filling.
  4. In a pan, heat 1 tbsp oil, add mustard seeds, curry leaves, chopped onion, green chilies, and turmeric powder.
  5. Add mashed potatoes and salt, mix well, and cook for 2–3 minutes; set aside the masala.
  6. Heat a dosa tawa, pour a ladle of batter, and spread it thin in a circular motion.
  7. Drizzle some oil or ghee around the edges and cook until golden brown.
  8. Place the potato masala in the center, fold the dosa, and cook for a few more seconds.
  9. Serve hot with coconut chutney and sambar.

Delhi's Chole Bhature

🍲 Cooking Instructions

  1. Soak 1 cup white chickpeas overnight, then pressure cook with salt until soft but not mushy.
  2. Heat 2 tbsp oil in a pan, add chopped onions and ginger-garlic paste, sauté till golden brown.
  3. Add tomato puree, red chili powder, coriander powder, cumin powder, and chole masala; cook till oil separates.
  4. Add boiled chickpeas along with a little of their water, mix well, and simmer for 10–15 minutes.
  5. Add salt and a pinch of garam masala, cook till thick and flavorful.
  6. For Bhature, mix 2 cups maida, 2 tbsp semolina, ½ tsp baking powder, ¼ cup curd, and water to make a soft dough.
  7. Rest the dough for 2–3 hours, then divide into balls and roll into circles.
  8. Deep fry in hot oil until golden and puffed.
  9. Serve hot Chole with Bhature, onion slices, and lemon wedges.

Chettinad Chicken Varuval

🍲 Cooking Instructions

  1. Clean and cut 500g chicken into medium pieces; keep aside.
  2. Dry roast 1 tsp fennel seeds, 1 tsp cumin seeds, 1 tbsp coriander seeds, 6–8 dry red chilies, and few curry leaves; grind them into a coarse powder.
  3. Heat 3 tbsp oil in a pan, add chopped onions and curry leaves, and sauté until golden brown.
  4. Add 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste and 1 chopped tomato; cook until soft and oil separates.
  5. Add the ground Chettinad masala, ¼ tsp turmeric, and salt; sauté for a minute.
  6. Add chicken pieces and mix well so the masala coats them evenly.
  7. Pour ½ cup water, cover with a lid, and cook on medium flame for 10–15 minutes until chicken turns tender.
  8. Remove the lid and cook on high flame for a few minutes until the gravy thickens and turns dry (varuval style).
  9. Garnish with fresh curry leaves and serve hot with rice, dosa, or parotta.

Gobi Manchurian Fried Rice

🍲 Cooking Instructions

  1. Cut cauliflower (gobi) into small florets, blanch in hot water for 2 minutes, then drain completely.
  2. Make a batter with maida, cornflour, salt, pepper, and a little water; coat the florets and deep fry until golden and crisp.
  3. Heat 2 tbsp oil in a wok, add chopped garlic, onion, capsicum, and spring onions; stir-fry for 2 minutes.
  4. Add 1 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tbsp chili sauce, 1 tbsp tomato ketchup, and ½ tsp vinegar; mix well.
  5. Add cooked and cooled rice to the wok and toss everything together on high flame.
  6. Add salt, pepper, and the fried gobi pieces; mix gently so the florets stay crisp.
  7. Sprinkle spring onion greens and toss one last time.
  8. Serve hot with extra chili sauce or raita on the side.

Chilli Paneer Hakka Noodles

🍲 Cooking Instructions

  1. Boil 200g Hakka noodles in salted water until just cooked (al dente), then drain and toss with a little oil.
  2. Heat 2 tbsp oil in a wok, add diced paneer cubes, and shallow fry until golden; set aside.
  3. In the same wok, add chopped garlic, spring onions, sliced onions, capsicum, and carrots; sauté on high flame for 2–3 minutes.
  4. Add 1 tbsp soy sauce, 1 tbsp chili sauce, 1 tsp vinegar, and 1 tbsp tomato ketchup; mix well.
  5. Add the fried paneer and boiled noodles to the wok, toss everything together quickly on high heat.
  6. Season with salt, pepper, and a pinch of sugar for balance.
  7. Sprinkle spring onion greens and mix gently.
  8. Serve hot for a perfect Indo-Chinese meal.

Szechuan Dosa

🍲 Cooking Instructions

  1. Prepare dosa batter by soaking rice and urad dal, then grind and ferment overnight.
  2. Heat 1 tbsp oil in a pan, add chopped onions, capsicum, cabbage, and carrots; sauté on high flame.
  3. Add 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a few seconds.

  7. Spread a spoon of Szechuan sauce over the dosa and place the prepared veggie mixture in the center.

  9. Serve hot with coconut chutney or extra Szechuan sauce.

Paneer Bhurji Burritos

Rajma Masala Nachos

🍲 Cooking Instructions

  1. Soak 1 cup rajma (kidney beans) overnight and pressure cook until soft.

Tandoori Chicken Malai Pizza

🍲 Cooking Instructions

  1. Marinate 300g boneless chicken with ½ cup curd, 1 tbsp ginger-garlic paste, 1 tsp red chili powder, 1 tsp tandoori masala, salt, and 1 tbsp lemon juice; rest for 1 hour.
  2. Grill or pan-fry chicken pieces until cooked and slightly charred; keep aside.
  3. Spread pizza base with white malai sauce (mix of cream, garlic, salt, and pepper) or regular pizza sauce.
  4. Add a layer of mozzarella cheese and place the tandoori chicken pieces on top.
  5. Sprinkle sliced onions, capsicum, and chili flakes.
  6. Bake in a preheated oven at 200°C (392°F) for 10–12 minutes or until the cheese melts.
  7. Brush edges with butter or ghee for a desi touch.
  8. Garnish with coriander or mint leaves and serve hot.

Gulab Jamun Cheesecake

🍲 Cooking Instructions

  1. Crush 15–20 biscuits (like Marie or Digestive) and mix with 4 tbsp melted butter; press into a cake tin to form the base.
  2. Refrigerate the base for 15–20 minutes to set.
